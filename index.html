📖 Portal Playbook

Everything you need to read the numbers with confidence.

This is your plain-English field guide to the analytics portal. No jargon, no guesswork — just what each report means, which filter changes what, and how to turn a screen full of numbers into a decision. Skim it once and you'll know your way around every tab.

🚀 Start here — the portal in 3 moves

The whole portal follows one simple rhythm. Once it clicks, every tab works the same way.

① Set your filters

At the very top sits the filter bar — a date range, a client, the products, and a store-count mode. Think of it as the "lens" you look through. Set it once and it follows you across the tabs.

② Pick a tab

Each tab answers a different question: How are sales? What's the inventory like? What should we re-order? Where are we headed? Choose the tab that matches the question on your mind.

③ Read the report

Every report has a small coloured tag under its title telling you which filters it listens to. Green is good, red needs attention, amber means "soon". Colour does the heavy lifting — let it guide your eye.

💡

The golden rule: if a number ever looks surprising, check the filter bar first. 9 times out of 10 the data is simply being shown through a narrower lens (a single client, a short date range, or a few products) than you expected.

🎛️ The filter bar, explained

These controls live at the top of the screen and shape almost everything below them. Here's what each one does — and a tip for using it well.

📅 From / To dates

Sets the time window for the data. Everything date-aware only counts sales that fall between these two dates.

Tip: Widen the range to spot long-term trends; narrow it to focus on a recent quarter or promotion.

🏢 Client

Focus the whole portal on one account, or leave it on All Clients to see the combined picture.

Tip: A couple of reports deliberately ignore this so you can compare accounts — they're flagged in the next section.

📦 Products

Pick one, many, or all products. Use the search box to find a SKU by code or name, then Select All / Clear All to act on what you've searched.

Tip: "Select All" only adds the products currently matching your search — so you can build a selection across several searches.

🏬 Store Count

Switches how "per-store" figures are calculated. Average uses the typical store count across the period; Latest uses the most recent week's store count.

Why it matters: If a product rolled out to more stores recently, "Latest" gives a fairer read of current per-store performance.
↺

Reset clears everything back to the full date range, all clients, all products, and Average store count — a clean slate whenever you feel lost. The ↻ Refresh button pulls the latest data from the source.

Year × Month MatrixQty & Value

⚙️ How replenishment setup works

The Replenishment Planning Report works backwards from the day you'd run out of stock. To do that, it needs to know how long each step in your supply chain takes. You set those once, per client, in Lead Time Configuration.

Setting (weeks)What it means
Replenishment Arrival BufferA safety cushion — extra weeks of stock you want on hand when the new order lands, so you're never cutting it fine.
Transit Lead TimeHow long goods take to travel from the factory/warehouse to the client.
Production Lead TimeHow long it takes to actually make the product after an order is placed.
PO Processing Lead TimeThe admin time to raise and confirm a purchase order before production begins.

The planner adds these up and counts backwards from "stock lasts till" to give you four dates: the latest PO Receive By, Production Start, Expected Dispatch and Replenishment Arrival. Miss the earliest one and you risk a stock-out — that's why past-due dates turn red.

🧰 Extra filters on the planning report

Above the table you'll find three controls that fine-tune what's shown. They sit in one row, left to right:

Replenishable All · Yes · No

Shows only products marked as re-orderable (Yes), only the non-reorderable ones (No), or everything (All). Choosing Yes/No reshapes all the counts below it.

Stock Filter All · No Stock · Has Stock

Quickly isolate products that are out of stock vs those that still have stock. The number in each button updates with your other choices.

Stock Source All · Store · DC · Rush

Decides which stock counts toward "Total Stock": everything combined, or just store stock, distribution-centre stock, or rush-order stock. Handy when you only care about one location.

📚 Plain-English glossary

The handful of terms that show up across the portal. Tap any to expand.

Weeks of Cover (WOC)
How many weeks your current stock will last if sales carry on at the recent pace. Low WOC = you may run out soon; high WOC = cash tied up in stock that's selling slowly.
Sell-Through %
The share of available stock that actually sold. A high percentage means product is moving well relative to what was on hand. It's a great companion to raw sales — strong sales with weak sell-through can hide an overstock.
Units / Store / Week
Average units sold per store, per week. It strips out "we just have more stores" so you can compare productivity fairly. The store count used depends on your Average vs Latest choice.
Average vs Latest store count
Average uses the typical number of stores across the whole period; Latest uses the most recent week's store count applied across the range. If distribution changed recently, the two can differ noticeably — both are correct, they just answer slightly different questions.
Momentum (period-over-period)
Whether things are speeding up or slowing down compared with the previous comparable period. Shown as a green ▲ (improving) or red ▼ (declining) with a percentage.
YoY · YTD · CYTD · LYTD
YoY = Year-over-Year (this year vs last). YTD = Year-to-Date (Jan up to now). CYTD = Current Year-to-Date; LYTD = the same weeks Last Year-to-Date — so the comparison is fair, week-for-week.
Pareto / concentration
A way of seeing how much of your sales come from your top few products. If a small handful of SKUs make up most of the volume, you're "concentrated" — great when they're healthy, risky if one stumbles.
Total Stock — Store, DC & Rush
Stock lives in a few places: on the shop floor (Store), in the distribution centre (DC), and on a Rush order in motion. "Total Stock" adds them up; the Stock Source filter lets you look at just one.
❓ Quick troubleshooting

The questions that come up most often.

A report looks empty — what happened?
Almost always the filters are too narrow: a date range with no sales, a client that didn't sell the selected products, or a single SKU with no activity. Hit Reset to widen everything, then narrow again step by step.
I changed a filter but a number didn't move.
That report probably ignores that filter on purpose — check its coloured tag. For example, the Period-over-Period cards use their own period pickers (not the date range), and Client Yearly Performance always shows all clients.
Average and Latest store count give different answers.
That's expected and healthy. They use a different store count in the maths. Watch the Stores (Avg) / Stores (Latest) column header so you always know which one you're reading.
Why are some replenishment dates red or amber?
Red means the date has already passed — you're at risk of a stock-out. Amber means it's coming up within 14 days. Green/plain dates have comfortable runway.
How do I compare two accounts fairly?
Use Client Yearly Performance (Sales tab) or the Account contribution chart (AI tab) — both show every account at once regardless of the Client filter.
How fresh is the data?
Press the ↻ Refresh button at the top to pull the latest figures from the source.
